Crispy Pork Sisig

Description

Sisig (/ˈsiːsɪɡ/ Tagalog pronunciation: ['sisig]) is a Filipino dish made from pork jowl and ears (maskara), pork belly, and chicken liver, which is usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It originates from the Pampanga region in Luzon. Kapampangan sisig served on a hot plate

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork face (maskara), boiled and grilled, then chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 bird's eye chilies (siling labuyo), chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons liver spread or pâté
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges

Steps

  1. Boil the pork until tender, then grill until crispy and chop into small pieces.
  2. Sauté the garlic and onion until softened.
  3. Add the chopped pork and cook until heated through.
  4. Stir in the soy sauce, vinegar, and ground black pepper.
  5. Add the liver spread and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Mix in the mayonnaise and chopped chilies.
  7. Serve hot with an optional fried egg on top and calamansi or lemon wedges on the side.
